01. LIVING WITH ADHD

Why This Website Has a Focus Mode

I have ADHD (Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder), and it profoundly shapes how I interact with technology, information, and the world around me. Building this website gave me the opportunity to create something that works for my brain‚Äîand hopefully helps others like me.

üíú What ADHD Means for Me

  • Hyperfocus vs. Distraction: I can spend 12 hours straight debugging a network issue, but reading a simple article can feel impossible with too many visual distractions.
  • Information Overload: Busy layouts, excessive animations, and cluttered designs make my brain feel like it's trying to process everything at once.
  • Executive Dysfunction: Sometimes the simplest tasks require enormous mental energy, especially when interfaces aren't clear and intuitive.
  • Sensory Sensitivity: Bright colors, rapid movements, and visual noise can be overwhelming and exhausting.

The Focus Mode button (üß†) in the header isn't just an accessibility feature‚Äîit's a reflection of my lived experience. When activated, it:

üåü Why I'm Sharing This

ADHD is often invisible. People see the hyperfocus on tech projects but don't see the struggle with "simple" tasks. They see the creative problem-solving but don't see the executive dysfunction. By building accessibility into this site and talking openly about it, I hope to:

  • Help others with ADHD feel seen and understood
  • Demonstrate that accessibility benefits everyone, not just those with diagnosed conditions
  • Encourage other developers to consider neurodiversity in their designs
  • Normalize talking about ADHD in tech spaces

"If this Focus Mode helps even one person with ADHD navigate the web more comfortably, then it's worth it."
